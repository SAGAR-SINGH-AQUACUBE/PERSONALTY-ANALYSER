Extraversion (E) vs. Introversion (I): Determines whether you draw energy from the outer world of people and activities (Extraversion) or from your inner world of thoughts and reflections (Introversion). Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N): Describes how you gather information. Sensors rely on their five senses and prefer concrete, tangible data, while Intuitives focus on patterns, connections, and possibilities. Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F): Reflects how you make decisions. Thinkers prioritize logic, objectivity, and consistency, while Feelers consider emotions, empathy, and personal values. Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P): Concerns how you orient yourself to the external world. Judgers prefer structure, organization, and closure, while Perceivers are more flexible, adaptable, and open-ended.
